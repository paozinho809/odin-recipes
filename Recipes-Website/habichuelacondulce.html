Habichuela Con Dulce

What is it?

This dish is a delicacy in the Dominican Republic. It is a dessert that consists of beans, milk, coconut milk, sweet potatoes, raisins and spices. It's typically had around lent

Ingredients

  • 4 cup boiled red kidney beans
  • 6 cups of water
  • 2 cups of coconut milk
  • 3 cups of evaporated milk
  • 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 cup sugar (white, granulated)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 10 cloves
  • 1/2 pound sweet potato
  • 1/2 cup raisins

Garnish with:

  • 8 pieces casabe
  • 2 teaspoons butter(salted)
  • 1 cup milk cookies
  1. Put the beans (and the water in which they boiled) in a blender and puree. Strain the beans to get rid of the skins and undissolved solids.
  2. Boiling:

    Pour the beans, coconut milk, evaporated milk, salt, sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, cloves, and sweet potatoes into a pot.Simmer over very low heat until the sweet potatoes are cooked through. Stir regularly to avoid sticking.

    Simmer over very low heat until the sweet potatoes are cooked through. Stir regularly to avoid sticking.

    Add the raisins and simmer for another 10 minutes (don't worry that it may look too thin, the cream of beans will get much thicker when chilled). Remove the cinnamon sticks (and cloves, if you like).

  3. Chilling:Remove from the heat and cool to room temperature. Chill before serving.
  4. Making casabe:Spread butter on the cassava bread and toast in the oven until it turns golden brown.
  5. Serving:Serve the beans with the cassava on the side. Put cookies in the beans when you serve.
